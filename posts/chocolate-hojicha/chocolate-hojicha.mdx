<StyledParagraph>Decadent chocolate cake paired with a hojicha infused cream makes for a sophisticated dessert. The more robust and deeper essence of this tea can stand up to the chocolate and balances it out perfectly.</StyledParagraph>

| prep | cook | servings | level |
| :--: | :--: | :------: | :---: |
| 20 min | 40 min |   6  | Intermediate  |

## Ingredients

- <ListItem>50g butter</ListItem>
- <ListItem>55g heavy cream</ListItem>
- <ListItem>74g dark chocolate</ListItem>
- <ListItem>15g cocoa powder</ListItem>
- <ListItem>25g AP flour</ListItem>
- <ListItem>3 </ListItem>
- <ListItem>50g of sugar, divided</ListItem>

## Cream
- <ListItem>250g heavy cream</ListItem>
- <ListItem>6g hojicha tea</ListItem>


## Steps

1. Melt chocolate and butter in a double boiler and let cool to room temperature
2. In another bowl, whip egg yolks and 10g of sugar until pale yellow (5min)
3. Stir in the cream into the egg yolk mixture. Add in the melted chocolate and stir
4. Sift in cocoa powder and flour into the mixture and mix until well incorporated
5. In a seperate bowl, whip the egg whites until foamy and slowly add 40g of sugar. Continue mixing until stiff peaks
6. Fold the egg whites into the other bowl with the rest of the batter in 2 additions. Pour into a 6inch cake pan and bake at 350 deg F for 35-40min
7. For the cream, heat 150g of cream until just before boiling. Turn off the heat and place tea leaves into the pot and let steep for 10min. Strain the cream to remove the tea leaves and place in the fridge until cold (30min)
8. In a bowl, measure 100g of cream and add the infused cream. Whip the cream until soft peaks.
9. Ice the cake and let set for 2h before cutting and serving
