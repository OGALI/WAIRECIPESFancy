<StyledParagraph>Afternoon time tea can no longer go without this asian inspired Swiss roll cake. Fluffy and moist cake with a creamy and decadent filling. This will go down so easy you'll need to make double!</StyledParagraph>

| prep | cook | servings | level |
| :--: | :--: | :------: | :---: |
| 20 min | 12 min |  1 roll cake  | Intermediate  |

## Ingredients

- <ListItem>3 egg yolks</ListItem>
- <ListItem>10g sugar</ListItem>
- <ListItem>20ml oil</ListItem>
- <ListItem>40ml milk</ListItem>
- <ListItem>53g cake flour</ListItem>
- <ListItem>7g matcha</ListItem>
- <ListItem>3 egg white</ListItem>
- <ListItem>50g sugar</ListItem>
- <ListItem>Red bean filling (<a href="https://www.justonecookbook.com/pressure-cooker-anko-red-bean-paste/" target="_blank"  rel="noreferrer noopener">here</a>)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>Cream (120 ml heavy cream + 1tbsp granulated sugar)</ListItem> 

## Preparation

1. Whisk egg yolks and sugar until the mixture turns pale yellow (5 min)
2. Stir in the oil and milk. Once incorporated, sift in the flour and matcha 
3. In a seperate bowl, whisk eggwhites until foamy and gradually add in the sugar until stiff peaks 
4. Fold in the eggwhites into the matcha batter in two additions until smooth 
5. Bake in a 9x13 inch pan at 350 deg F for 10-12 min 
6. Once the cake is cooled to room temperature, spread the cream evenly onto the cake. With a pipping bag, pipe the red bean in 3 long strips
7. Roll the cake along the shorter end and refrigerate for at least 30min to set the cake
