<StyledParagraph>Korean style dumpling filled with savory juicy pork balanced with fresh kimchi and wrapped in a chewy homemade dumpling skin. These are fun to make, but dangerously easy to finish a full plate. Try out this recipe if you are looking to expand your dumpling flavor repertoire!</StyledParagraph>

| prep | cook | servings | level |
| :--: | :--: | :------: | :---: |
| 2 hours | 10 min | 4 | Intermediate  |

## Ingredients

## Filling 
- <ListItem>2 ounces dangmyeon sweet potato starch noodles, soaked in warm water for about 30 min</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 cup packed finely chopped kimchi</ListItem>
- <ListItem>8 ounces tofu</ListItem>
- <ListItem>8 ounces mung bean sprouts</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 medium onion</ListItem>
- <ListItem>2 to 3 scallions</ListItem>
- <ListItem>8 ounces ground pork or beef (or mix)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp minced garlic</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 tsp finely grated ginger or juiced</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp sesame oil</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp soy sauce</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp gochugaru (adjust to taste)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>salt to taste about 1/4 teaspoon</ListItem>
- <ListItem>pepper to taste about ⅛ teaspoon</ListItem>

## Wrapper 
- <ListItem>2 cups all purpose flour plus ¼ cup extra for dusting</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 tsp kosher salt</ListItem>
- <ListItem>2/3 cup water</ListItem>


## Steps
1. For the wrappers, combine flour, kosher salt, and water in a large mixing bowl.
2. Mix with a wooden spoon until it turns into a lump. Then knead it by hand for a few minutes until the dough gets a little softer. Let the dough rest for at least 30 minutes. 
3. For the filling, Finely chop the kimchi and squeeze out excess liquid by hand.
4. Blanch the bean sprouts in boiling water, drain, chop and squeeze out water.
5. Squeeze out water from the tofu. Finely chop the noodles. Finely chop the onion and squeeze out water. Finely chop the scallions.
6. Combine all the filling ingredients together until well incorporated. 
7. Place one heaping tablespoon of filling into a wrapper. Wet the edges of the wrapper with water or egg wash and seal tightly (pushing the air out with your fingers) into a half-moon shape. Then, bring the two ends together, apply water or egg wash to one end and press tightly to create a round shape. Repeat this process until all the filling/wrappers are used.
8. Steam for about 10 minutes in a steamer (12 minutes if frozen).
