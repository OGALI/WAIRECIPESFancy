<StyledParagraph>This dessert taste exactly like a milk tea! You can use any of your favourite teas for this panna cotta.</StyledParagraph>

| prep | cook | servings | level |
| :--: | :--: | :------: | :---: |
| 15 min | overnight (fridge) |  4  | Easy  |

## Ingredients

- <ListItem>3/4 cup whole milk (180 ml)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>2 cups heavy cream (500 ml)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/4 cup + 1 tbsp white or brown sugar (63 gr)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>3 gelatin sheets (4.5g)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 tsp vanilla extract</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/4 cup Assam black tea or your favourite tea</ListItem>

## Brown Sugar Syrup 
- <ListItem>1/4 cup brown sugar (50 gr)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/4 cup water (60 ml)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 tsp cornstarch</ListItem>

## Steps
1. Place gelatin sheets in water until soft.
2. Heat milk and cream until it boils
3. Turn off the heat
4. Add brown sugar, vanilla, tea and softened gelatin sheets
5. Let tea steep for 15-30mins
6. Strain mixture
7. Place in mold
8. Let cool in fridge overnight
9. For the brown sugar syrup, heat sugar, water and cornstarch in a pot
10. Let boil and turn off heat
11. To remove from the mold, dip the bottom of the mold into a pot of hot water for 5 seconds to loosen the panna cotta. Slide a knife around the edge, then carefully turn it over onto a serving plate.
12. Serve with brown sugar syrup if desired
