<StyledParagraph>This recipe is great for most of us who need to use up over ripe bananas towards the end of the week. If you want to try something different and change up your traditional banana bread recipe, miso is the perfect addition. The hint of salt from the miso balances out the sweetness of the bananas.</StyledParagraph>

| prep | cook | servings | level |
| :--: | :--: | :------: | :---: |
| 20 min | 50 min |  1 loaf  | Easy  |

## Ingredients

- <ListItem>3 overripe bananas, plus one more for garnish</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 cup unsalted butter</ListItem>
- <ListItem>3 tablespoons shiro miso</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/4 cup granulated sugar</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 cup brown sugar</ListItem>
- <ListItem>2 whole eggs</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 teaspoon vanilla extract</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 tablespoon baking powder</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 teaspoon cinnamon</ListItem>

## Steps
1. Mash the bananas in a large bowl
2. In another bowl, mix the room temperature butter, miso and sugars until light and fluffy. Add in the bananas and eggs and mix until well incorporated.
3. Add the dry ingredients into the batter and mix until smooth.
4. Poor into a loaf pan and top with one banana sliced lengthwise. Bake in the oven at 350 deg F for 45-50 minutes until tooth pick inserted comes out clean
