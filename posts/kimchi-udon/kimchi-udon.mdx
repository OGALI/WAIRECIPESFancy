<StyledParagraph>Super simple and quick stir fry packed with kimchi and full of probiotics. Recipe inspired by <a href="https://mykoreankitchen.com/kimchi-udon-noodle-stir-fry/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">mykoreankitchen</a></StyledParagraph>

| prep | cook | servings | level |
| :--: | :--: | :------: | :---: |
| 15 min | 10 min |  2  | Easy  |

# Ingredients

- <ListItem>2 packs udon noodles (400g/14 ounces)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 cup aged Kimchi, chopped into thumbnail size pieces</ListItem>
- <ListItem>50g/ 1.8 ounces onion, thinly sliced</ListItem>
- <ListItem>30g/ 1 ounce green onion, thinly sliced</ListItem>

## Sauce 
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp Korean chilli flakes (gochugaru)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 Tbsp Korean chilli paste (gochujang)</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp soy sauce</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp maple syrup</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp raw sugar</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1/2 tsp minced garlic</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 tsp sesame oil</ListItem>

## Garnish
- <ListItem>2 eggs, soft boiled or pan-fried</ListItem>
- <ListItem>A few sprinkles of ground black pepper</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp roasted sesame seeds</ListItem>
- <ListItem>1 Tbsp green onion, thinly sliced</ListItem>

# Steps

1. Boil udon as per instructions on the package 
2. Saute kimchi and vegetables in a pan with oil until fragrant on med-high heat. Add the sauce and cook for 1-2 min
3. Stir fry noodles in the pan and serve with the garnishes
